# Growth Equity Investor Playbook

## Core Investment Thesis Framework

### What We Look For
1. **Product-Market Fit Evidence**: >$5M ARR with clear repeatable sales motion
2. **Capital Efficiency**: <$1.50 burn multiple (ideally <$1.00)
3. **Growth Durability**: Path to T2D3 (triple, triple, double, double, double)
4. **Large TAM**: $10B+ addressable market with credible expansion path
5. **Defensibility**: Network effects, switching costs, or data moats

### Deal Killers
- Burn multiple >2.5x
- Gross retention <80%
- Customer concentration >30% from top 3
- Founder misalignment on exit timeline
- Undifferentiated product in crowded market

## GTM Efficiency Metrics

### Magic Number / Sales Efficiency
```
Magic Number = (QoQ ARR Growth * 4) / Prior Quarter S&M Spend
```
- >1.0: Excellent - increase S&M spend
- 0.75-1.0: Good - efficient growth
- 0.5-0.75: Okay - optimize before scaling
- <0.5: Concerning - fix unit economics first

- Excellent: >5x

### Rule of 40 Impact
```
Rule of 40 = Revenue Growth % + FCF Margin %
```
- >60: Premium multiple (1.5-2x normal)
- 40-60: Standard multiple
- 20-40: Discount (0.5-0.75x normal)
- <20: Deep discount (<0.5x normal)

## Business Model Pattern Recognition

### PLG (Product-Led Growth)
**Signs of Good PLG:**
- Viral coefficient >1
- <$1K ACV but high volume
- Self-serve onboarding
- Clear upgrade triggers
- Strong organic growth (>30% of new ARR)

**PLG Red Flags:**
- High support costs despite self-serve claims
- Low activation rates (<25%)
- Long time-to-value (>7 days)
- Conversion rate <3% free-to-paid

### Sales-Led Enterprise
**Signs of Healthy Motion:**
- ACV >$100K
- Win rates >30%
- Sales cycle <120 days
- SDR:AE ratio 1:2 or better
- Clear expansion playbook

**Enterprise Red Flags:**
- Single-threaded champions
- Long sales cycles (>180 days)
- Heavy customization requirements
- Services >20% of revenue

### Usage-Based Pricing
**What to Verify:**
- Gross retention (more important than NRR)
- Usage correlation with business outcomes
- Predictability of customer usage
- Committed revenue vs. usage revenue mix

**Usage-Based Pitfalls:**
- Inflated NRR masking churn
- Customer budget exhaustion
- Commodity pricing pressure
- Unpredictable revenue

## Due Diligence Questions by Topic

### Unit Economics Deep Dive
1. "Walk me through your fully-loaded CAC calculation"
2. "What's your gross retention by cohort over 24 months?"
3. "How does NRR break down between expansion and churn?"
4. "What's driving the delta between logo retention and dollar retention?"

### GTM Motion
1. "What percentage of pipeline is inbound vs. outbound?"
2. "How has win rate trended over the last 4 quarters?"
3. "What's your average sales cycle and how has it changed?"
4. "Walk me through your typical land-and-expand motion"

### Competitive Positioning
1. "How do customers solve this today without you?"
2. "Who do you lose deals to and why?"
3. "What would it take for a customer to switch away from you?"
4. "Where are you on the Gartner Magic Quadrant trajectory?"

### Capital Efficiency
1. "What's your burn multiple over the last 12 months?"
2. "How much ARR did you add per $1M of equity raised?"
3. "What would you do differently with 50% less capital?"
4. "What's your path to cash flow break-even?"

### Team & Org
1. "Who are your top 3 hires in the last year and why?"
2. "What's your voluntary attrition and how does it compare to industry?"
3. "How has the management team evolved since founding?"
4. "What's the next critical executive hire?"

## Red Flags Checklist

### Financial Red Flags
- [ ] Revenue recognition issues (upfront payments, multi-year deals)
- [ ] Burn accelerating faster than growth
- [ ] Declining gross margins
- [ ] High accounts receivable / long DSO
- [ ] Customer concentration
- [ ] Inconsistent metric definitions
- [ ] "Adjusted" metrics that exclude real costs

### GTM Red Flags
- [ ] Declining win rates
- [ ] Lengthening sales cycles
- [ ] High sales turnover
- [ ] Dependent on channel partners
- [ ] Single vertical dependency
- [ ] Heavy discounting to close deals

### Product Red Flags
- [ ] Heavy services requirements
- [ ] Long implementation times
- [ ] High support ticket volume
- [ ] Feature bloat without usage
- [ ] Tech debt limiting velocity
- [ ] No clear product roadmap

### Market Red Flags
- [ ] "No competition" (usually means no market)
- [ ] Regulatory risk
- [ ] Platform dependency (AWS, Salesforce, etc.)
- [ ] Market timing questions
- [ ] Unclear buyer persona

## Negotiation Framework

### Key Terms to Watch
1. **Liquidation Preference**: Target 1x non-participating
2. **Anti-dilution**: Weighted average preferred
3. **Board Composition**: Maintain founder control pre-profitability
4. **Protective Provisions**: Standard list, no overreach
5. **Pro-rata Rights**: Super pro-rata is a flag
6. **Redemption**: Avoid or push out 7+ years

### Valuation Negotiation
- Anchor to public comps with growth adjustment
- Reference recent private transactions
- Build DCF as sanity check
- Consider secondary / liquidity expectations
- Factor in structural elements (participating preferred, etc.)

## Quick Mental Models

### The 3 Ps
- **People**: Would I want to work for this founder?
- **Product**: Would I buy this if I were the target customer?
- **Potential**: Can this be a $1B+ outcome?

### The Regret Test
- Would I regret passing on this if it 10x'd?
- Would I regret investing if it went to zero?

### The Replacement Test
- If the CEO left, could we recruit a replacement?
- If a competitor launched this tomorrow, would customers switch?

### The 10x Test
- For this to 10x, what has to go right?
- Is that a market shift or execution question?
